THE DIEPPE RAID

August 19, 1942: A Fateful Day for Canada in WWII

Discover the Story

The Mission

Date & Location

August 19, 1942, on the heavily fortified beaches of Dieppe, France.

Objectives & Strategy

  • Test German coastal defenses and capture the port.
  • Gather vital intelligence for future landings.
  • Boost Allied morale and demonstrate capability.
  • Divert German resources from the Eastern Front.
  • Strategy: A frontal assault on the main port (Red and White beaches), supported by flanking attacks on Pourville (Green beach) and Puys (Blue beach), along with commando raids on artillery batteries.

Key Events of the Raid

03:48 - Ill-fated Encounter

Landing craft carrying No. 3 Commando run into a small German coastal convoy. The ensuing firefight alerts German shore defenses prematurely.

04:50 - Landings Begin

Troops begin landing. At Puys (Blue Beach), the Royal Regiment of Canada faces intense, accurate fire and suffers devastating casualties within minutes. At Pourville (Green Beach), the South Saskatchewan Regiment and Queen's Own Cameron Highlanders initially meet lighter resistance.

05:20 - Main Assault

The main Canadian forces (Essex Scottish, Royal Hamilton Light Infantry) land on Red and White beaches in front of Dieppe. They are met with overwhelming machine gun and mortar fire. Tanks of the Calgary Regiment are delayed and struggle on the chert-stone beach.

09:00 - Situation Critical

Most objectives are unmet. Communication breaks down. Tanks are largely ineffective, either destroyed or unable to penetrate town. Casualties mount rapidly.

11:00 - Withdrawal Order

With the situation untenable, the Force Commanders issue the order to withdraw. Evacuation under heavy fire proves extremely difficult and costly.

14:00 - Raid Concludes

The last boats depart, leaving behind many dead, wounded, and those forced to surrender. The raid ends in a clear tactical defeat for the Allies.

The Outcome & Human Cost

The Dieppe Raid was a catastrophic failure in terms of its immediate objectives. The majority of the landing force was either killed, wounded, or captured.

Canada's Role & Enduring Significance

A Predominantly Canadian Force

The Dieppe Raid was overwhelmingly a Canadian operation. Of the nearly 6,100 Allied troops involved in the main landings, almost 5,000 were Canadian soldiers from the 2nd Canadian Infantry Division. This immense contribution underscored Canada's commitment to the Allied war effort.

Lessons Forged in Fire

Despite the tragic losses, the Dieppe Raid provided invaluable, albeit harsh, lessons for the Allies:

  • The need for overwhelming firepower and specialized equipment for amphibious assaults.
  • Improved intelligence and planning for landings on defended coasts.
  • The importance of air superiority and naval bombardment.
  • Better communication and coordination between land, sea, and air forces.

These lessons were crucial in the planning and successful execution of later Allied landings, most notably D-Day in Normandy in 1944.

The sacrifice at Dieppe remains a somber and significant chapter in Canadian military history, a testament to bravery in the face of overwhelming odds, and a hard-won step on the path to Allied victory.
